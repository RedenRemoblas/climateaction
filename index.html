Understanding Climate Change

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures have risen by about 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, with the last decade being the warmest on record. This warming is primarily caused by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels.

Rising Sea Levels

Sea levels are rising at a rate of 3.3mm per year due to thermal expansion and melting ice caps. This threatens coastal communities and small island nations worldwide.

Extreme Weather

Climate change is intensifying extreme weather events, including hurricanes, droughts, floods, and heatwaves, leading to devastating impacts on communities and ecosystems.

Ecosystem Impact

Many species are struggling to adapt to rapid climate changes, leading to shifts in habitats, altered migration patterns, and increased risk of extinction.

Solutions and Actions

Renewable Energy

Transitioning to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power can significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Sustainable Living

Adopting sustainable practices in daily life, including reducing waste, conserving energy, and making eco-friendly choices.

Forest Conservation

Protecting and restoring forests helps absorb carbon dioxide and maintain biodiversity.

Green Transportation

Using public transport, electric vehicles, and cycling can reduce carbon emissions from transportation.
